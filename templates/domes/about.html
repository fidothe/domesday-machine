About

This is a project in its very early stages. Using freely available academic data, we have created a map of the settlements shown in Domesday Book. You can search for places near you, and see their population, size and owners in 1086.

We are now hoping to create the first freely available English translation of Domesday Book, using a transcription of the Latin text, with help from volunteers. A free translation would have many uses for scholars and the public.

If you are interested in helping to translate the text for your local area, please get in touch.

About the data

Domesday Book was transcribed and geocoded during the 1990s by a team led by Professor John Palmer at the University of Hull. This was funded by the AHRC, and assisted by a copy of the text provided by the publishers Phillimore & Co. There is more about the history of this project at the Domesday Explorer website.

This site was built in 2010 using that freely available data by Anna Powell-Smith. Professor Palmer's original data (which includes statistics and geolocations for every place in Domesday) can be downloaded from the Arts & Humanities Data Service, in CSV or Access format. In time, this site should also expose the data in JSON via an API.
